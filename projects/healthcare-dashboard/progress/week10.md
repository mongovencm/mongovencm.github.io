# Week 10

- What did you do last week?
  
This past week, I adding a few new visualizations that compare data across different datasets. I added comparison graphs for training load versus fatigue and training load versus readiness, as well as sleep quality vs fatigue. 

- What do you plan to do this week?

This week, I plan to improve the usability of the dashboard by making the visualizations easier to interpret. Due to the amount of data points collected over two years of time, the graphs right now are rather cluttered and difficult to identify meaningful trends. I have been thinking about some approaches to make them easier to interpret. Some ideas to experiment with are aggregating data into weekly or monthly averages, adding rolling averages to smooth out fluctuations, displaying summary statistics alongside charts, and implementing more flexible date filtering so users can focus on specific time periods. Implementing improvements like these should make the dashboard more useful for identifying patterns and relationships.

- Are there any impediments in your way?

There are no major impediments, but choosing the best way to visualize the large amounts of time series data will require some experimentation. 

- Reflection on the process you used last week, how can you make the process work better?

I think the process I have been following continues to work well. Cleaning and organizing the datasets before building the dashboard has made it much easier to add new visualizations and comparisons without repeatedly modifying the raw data. As I begin testing out ways to display the data in more useful ways, I will also determine if there are additional preprocessing or organization improvements that would be beneficial to make in the cleaning scripts rather than in the application itself.
